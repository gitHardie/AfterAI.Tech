# No Vacancy

<img src="https://spectrum.ieee.org/media-library/glowing-stars-fill-a-pink-lined-cube-against-a-black-background-stars-spill-outside-the-cube.jpg?id=61623182&amp;width=1245&amp;height=700&amp;coordinates=0%2C446%2C0%2C447" /><br /><br /><p>We forge ahead with scientific quests,</p><p>but even zealous efforts hit a wall</p><p>trying to make what nature most detests:</p><p>nothing at all.</p><p>It seems like such a little thing to do—</p><p>removing every molecule. We find</p><p>that though we displace almost all, a few</p><p>remain behind.</p><p>It takes attentive planning and robust</p><p>equipment in a lab to do the chore</p><p>of pumping vacuum pressure down to just</p><p>a millitorr.</p><p>The stalwart researcher persists and loses</p><p>sleep, but can’t reach perfection—I’m afraid</p><p>the universe still stubbornly refuses</p><p>to be unmade.</p><p>Even in deepest space, a cubic meter</p><p>contains some particles. We must assess</p><p>there is no void, although conditions teeter</p><p>on emptiness.</p><p>The quantum mysteries will vex and weary</p><p>the brightest mind, the sharpest physicist.</p><p>True nothingness, while wonderful in theory,</p><p>does not exist.</p>
